Intern (Mater graduation project)

Jan 2013 - Aug 2013
Phiips, Eindhoven, the Netherlands

In this project, an approach is proposed to study structural identifiability of an ultrasonic transducer and a method is developed to estimate transducer parameters by using transducer impedances to provide information on transducer quality. First, an equivalent circuit model for an ultrasonic transducer is shown. Second, the Laplace transform approach is extended and applied to analyze the structural identifiability of an ultrasonic transducer system. Structural identifiability of transducer parameters is studied to provide a guideline for the choice of parameters to be estimated. Finally, a Nonlinear Least Squares (NLS) method is developed to estimate the transducer parameters by fitting the simulated to the measured transducer impedances. The NLS method is applied to the parameter estimations of ultrasonic transducers with various parameters. The results show the robustness and accuracy of the method. In the project, MATLAB is extensively used for modeling, identifiability analysis and parameter estimation.
